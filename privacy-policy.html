Privacy Policy

HealthTrack SG Programme

This Privacy Policy (or "Policy") describes practices of Health Promotion Board (HPB, we, our or us) in connection with information we collect, use and disclose through "HealthTrack SG," a health and wellness programme featuring a mobile application ("HealthTrack SG App") used with a wearable device paired with the HealthTrack SG App (the "Programme") offered by HPB, in partnership with Google, ConnectedLife and Fullerton Health. The Programme digital health and wellness programme that empowers patients and their doctors to examine how lifestyle changes can help patients manage their chronic conditions. Through the programme, participants will be able to track their health progress, gain a holistic view of their health, and receive personalised guidance through the use of a wearable device and the App. This Programme is available to Singapore residents with a valid Singpass account who have agreed to the "Programme Participation Agreement" (or "PPA") in the HealthTrack SG Programme. The PPA is visible in the HealthTrack SG App. The PPA further sets out the collection, use and/or disclosure of all data and/or information which is collected under the Programme (known as "Programme Data").

OBy using the Programme and/or providing us with your information, you consent to our collection, use, and disclosure (including transfer) of your information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. Please do not provide us with any information if you do not accept this Policy.

What types of information do we collect?

We may collect two types of information when you use the HealthTrack SG App and if you participate in the Programme: Personal Information and Non-Personally Identifiable Information. When this Policy uses the term "Programme Data", this includes both Personal Information and Non-Personally Identifiable Information.

By "Personal Information," we mean data that is associated with an identifiable individual, such as a name, address or e-mail address. Personal Information includes, for example, personally identifiable Programme and demographic information. And in connection with participation in HealthTrack SG, it also includes personally identifiable wellness or fitness information, including various activities identified through the App on your wearable device.

By "Non-Personally-Identifiable Information," we mean information that is not associated with an identifiable individual. Non-Personally-Identifiable information may include information which would otherwise be Personal Information, but which has been de-identified or aggregated.

How do we acquire and use your information?

We acquire Programme Data when you use the HealthTrack SG App and through your participation in the Programme. When you use the HealthTrack SG App, we collect information about your use of it, such as time spent viewing your wellness plan. After you consent to participate in the Programme, Programme Data is collected through the HealthTrack SG App, your use of your wearable device and information you or your doctor record via the Onboarding Survey or during medical consultations. If you consent to participate in the Programme, the full listing of the categories of Programme Data that may be collected, used and/or disclosed as part of your participation in the Programme, including information collected through the HealthTrack SG App is described in the PPA.

Your Programme Data will never be sold or used to drive sale of any products. It may be used in accordance with the Terms of Use which may include using the information you share in support of the Programme, to adjudicate rewards, to identify and deliver messages that may be helpful to your health and wellness or otherwise of interest to you. Note that your Programme Data is stored in Singapore.

We may use "cookies" and other technologies such as pixel tags and web beacons. These technologies are used to track information such as the number of users, their frequency of use, and which parts of the website they have visited. While these technologies can tell us when you use the Programme and which pages you visit, it cannot read data off your hard disk or mobile device. You can choose to disable cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. We also collect information about usage of the HealthTrack SG App. This helps us better understand user behaviour.

We may collect, use, and disclose your Programme Data which includes your Personal Information, for the purposes listed below and, if you choose to participate in the Programme and/or you opt-in to share certain Programme Data, as otherwise described in the PPA:

a. providing you and other users with the services and functions of the Programme, including setting up your account and managing your use and access, informing you about service updates, and managing and providing rewards;

b. to identify and deliver messages that may be of interest to you

c. to identify correlations between Programme Data collected and messages delivered as part of the Programme;

d. to inform you of Programme updates and announcements, which you can manage through notifications or email preferences;

e. to inform you of changes to the Programme and deliver other important messages (e.g. invites to participate in HPB-led research studies);

f. to develop and refine the Programme;

g. to provide personalised experiences and set goals tailored to you;

h. to understand overall Programme effectiveness and/or its impact on the healthcare system in Singapore; and

i. assisting you with enquiries and obtaining your feedback

For what and to whom may we disclose your information?

We may disclose your Programme Data, which includes your Personal Information, to third parties for certain purposes as described above and as further described below. In all cases we will require the recipient to protect the Programme Data and use it only for the purpose it was provided consistent with this Privacy Policy and, if you are participating in the Programme. We may share your Programme Data which includes your Personal Information, if:

a. the recipient has been authorised to provide services on our behalf;

b. the recipient is our service provider who provides us with necessary services including but not limited to IT services, hosting and maintenance services, organising of our events, accounting, data analytics services, email messaging services, delivery services, handling of payment transactions, marketing, etc;

c. the recipient is our consultant or professional adviser including but not limited to accountants, lawyers and auditors;

d. you have specifically consented to such disclosure;

e. required by law, such as pursuant to a subpoena, regulatory oversight, or other legal process;

f. we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary (a) to protect our rights, the integrity of the Programme, or your safety or the safety of others, or (b) to detect, prevent or respond to fraud, intellectual property infringement, violations of our Terms of Use, violations of law, or other misuse of the Programme or HealthTrack SG App; and/or

g. whereby aggregated reporting data may be shared with the Government of the Republic of Singapore (under the care of Ministry of Health) for administrative purposes such as, evaluating programme effectiveness; however no individual level data or Personal Information will be provided in this instance.

We will not knowingly collect Personal Information from minors under the age of fifteen (15).

How do we protect your information?

The security, integrity, and confidentiality of your information are extremely important to us. We have implemented technical, administrative and physical security measures that are designed to protect your information from unauthorised access, disclosure, use and modification. We regularly review our security practices to consider appropriate new technology and methods. We also periodically subject the Programme to simulated intrusion tests and have developed comprehensive disaster recovery plans. However, please be aware that despite our best efforts, no security measures are perfect or impenetrable.

What happens if HPB suspends your use of HealthTrack SG?

As described in the Terms of Use and in the sole and absolute discretion of HPB, you may be blocked from accessing the full programme experience, and your account may enter a inactive state, including if you do not sign any subsequent new PPAs, or update to the newest version of HealthTrack SG.

If your account enters a inactive status, in the discretion of HPB, (i) certain features of HealthTrack SG may not be available, and (ii) any Programme Data already collected will continue to be stored and used as described in your current PPA. Additionally, certain HealthTrack SG App Engagement Data and/or Programme Data may still be collected, stored, and used under the PPA currently in effect in this inactive status to allow for the redemption of awards and for reactivation under the new PPA.

HPB reserves the right to withdraw you from the Programme and any Programme Data already collected will continue to be stored and used as described in your current PPA.

What happens if I no longer want to participate?

As stated in the PPA, you are free to change your mind and stop participating in the Programme at any time. You can withdraw from the Programme through the App by clicking "Delete Account" under My profile followed by Privacy Policy on your HealthTrack SG App or contacting HealthTrack SG support at the number listed below. Please note that simply uninstalling the HealthTrack SG App will not lead to deletion of your account or associated Programme Data.

You may also be withdrawn from the Programme if you engage in any practices that are found to violate the Programme Terms of Use.

If you withdraw from the Programme, no additional Programme Data will be collected about you, however, information that was previously collected about you may continue to be used as described in this Policy and this PPA.

After participant withdrawal, HPB will keep data in accordance with any relevant applicable law, and in compliance with legal guidelines, if any.
